6 cardio exercises that are a great alternative to running

Running is, without a shadow of a doubt, one of the most effective yet simple cardiovascular exercises. From strengthening joints to improving mood, running has an abundance of benefits to offer. But, it is a mere fact that running is tough when you want to run for more than a few minutes, and it can be more and more difficult for those who have ankle, foot, or knee conditions.

Cardio exercises for those who hate running

If you are among those who find running a difficult exercise, then here are some cardiovascular exercises that can get the job done.

Calisthenics

A bodyweight movement exercise that needs little or no equipment, calisthenics can be done anywhere in your living room or in your office. In addition to improving cardiovascular health, calisthenics helps by adding strength and stamina.

For those who want a full-body workout, they can include both lower and upper body moves and it will turn into a great workout for muscle improvement.

Parallel bars or rings can also help to make this a different variety of workouts.

Indoor cycling

Indoor cycling is available in all gyms and you can bring one home. When you incorporate sitting and standing positions while adjusting the resistance levels, indoor cycling can be a full-body workout and it is highly beneficial for your cardiovascular health.

To make indoor cycling a comfortable experience, adjust the seat height up to your hip bone.

Use cycling shoes and it will help in getting the right grip on pedals while providing you the right balance.

Jump rope

Here, all you need is enough space to swing the rope. It has been proven in research that 10 minutes of jump rope is equally effective as 30 minutes of jogging exercise. Moreover, jump rope is one of the best ways to improve your footwork and mind and body coordination.

Start with the basics if you are new to jump rope, body warm-up can be helpful to jump rope in the right way.

Once you are comfortable with the basic jump rope technique, you can add different kinds of variations that will be fun and effective to perform.

Moving planks

A great workout for adding strength to your core muscles, you can combine different motions with traditional planks. When you add different motions, it will help in increasing the effectiveness of your workout. Plank jack, army crawl, and mountain climber are a few of those variations that you can add.

Overhead loaded carries

Also known as farmer’s walks, loaded carries is a simple and effective workout. It is a great workout for muscle strength to your arms and the core

You can lift a kettlebell or dumbbell, weight needs to be sufficient to challenge you but not too heavy.

Hold the weight at your side in each arm one-by-one, and try the basic walk.

You can increase your walking speed to get your heart rate up.

Boxing and/or kickboxing

A good and effective boxing workout can be done from the comfort of your home, you don't really need to step into the ring.

Make use of some combinations such as crosses, jabs, and kicks, you can ask an online personal trainer to help you create the sequence and train you in the right way

You can add resistance by adding a dumbbell or using ankle weight.

The bottom line

These workouts are a great alternative to running and can boost your cardiovascular health. Give them a try at your home and also share with your near and dear ones.
