7 workouts that help in getting six-pack abs

Having six-pack abs is a dream of every man, for those who follow a workout routine rigorously as well as those who follow a fitness routine simply to look good. Six-pack abs are desirable due to many obvious reasons, it means low abdominal fat, it means you have a lean body, and more importantly, six-paks make you look sexy!

But, it is a mere fact that neither biceps nor six-pack abs grow on trees. They are built, and to do this, one needs to follow the right workout, the right diet, and an unwavering commitment to achieving this goal.

Top 7 workouts that help you build six-pack abs

Check out these exercises and see the results.

A simple yoga mat in your home is sufficient, you don't need additional equipment to do this. In general, it looks like a normal plank, but when done right, doing this should be a lot difficult. The position is exactly like a normal plan, but you have to engage every possible muscle with the goal to brace your abs hard.

Dead bug

Lay your face up on your mat, your arms should be kept in the air above your torso and your legs should be in the air with knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Try lowering your opposite arm and leg towards the floor, return to the center and repeat on the other side, this should be done in a slow and controlled fashion.

Hollow extension-to-cannonball

Get on your back and into a cannonball-type shape and now you need to hug your knees into your chest. Simultaneously you need to extend your legs and arms outwards so that you get into a hollow position this should be done while pressing your lower back to the floor. Enjoy 5 seconds of the hold before you curl up again, you can do 5 reps for one set.

Dumbbell side bend

Hold a single medium-weight dumbbell in your right hand, place your left hand behind your head. As far as you are comfortable, bend to your right side and switch side. For doing it in a safer manner, ensure that you have a healthy spine and you do it in the right way.

Barbell back squat

Stand on your feet and keep your feet shoulder-width apart, keep your toes slightly pointing outwards and now sit just like you are sitting on a chair and get back up. This exercise works directly on many muscles such as adding strength to your posterior chain muscles, hamstrings, and glutes.

Bird dog

With this exercise, your body gets control and stability, it's a total body workout that works the rectus abdomen, erector spinae, as well as gluts. Moreover, if you have back concerns, this exercise can help in relieving pain and discomfort.

Start with a tabletop position, keeping your hands under your shoulder and your knees under your hips, your goal is to engage your abdominal muscles. Your hips will remain parallel to the floor and you will raise your right arm and left leg. Gaze down at the floor and keep your chin tuck into your chest. Repeat with your left arm and right leg. You can do 8 to 12 reps and 2 to 3 sets of this exercise.

This workout targets the rectus abdomen, from your back and legs to your shoulders and core, this simple exercise works on various body muscles.
