Stretching before bed: Stretches that improve the quality of sleep!

In today's time and age where we're all surrounded by stress, nervousness, and different feelings of anxiety, falling asleep faster has become a hard nut to crack. We're all worried about the health and safety of our near and dear ones as pandemic has become a part of our life. We're all hoping for it to end soon so that we can keep masks and sensitizers away and live the hassle-free life that we used to before the crisis.

All these worries have made our daily life more difficult than ever before, and falling asleep is no exception, stretching before bed can make things easy.

Stretches before bed for a good night's sleep

Here are some stretches that can work wonders, and help you fall asleep faster.

Note: You can also consult an online personal trainer to help you do stretches the right way.

Neck stretching

These are some of those highly neglected stretches that are sure to help you find relief from head, neck, and shoulder pain. It is advisable to maintain good posture while performing these stretches, and you can feel free to take the help of your partner to do them with safety and ease.

  • Grab a chair and sit comfortably, keep your right hand on top of your head.
  • Now, without rushing, gently push your right ear towards your right shoulder, hold for 5 to 7 seconds.Do the same for the opposite side.
  • Do the same for the opposite side.
  • Another variation you can try.
  • Drop your chin down towards your chest and hold for 5 to 7 seconds.
  • Get back in the normal position and let your head gently fall back, hold for 5 to 7 seconds and repeat.

Child's pose

In addition to back and shoulders, this stretch is helpful for your hips, thighs, and growin. It is advisable not to push yourself too hard while performing this stretch.

  • For this, you need to come down on your knees and sit back on your heels.
  • Fold your body forward and your forehead should rest on the floor.
  • If you feel if you need some extra support, you can make use of a pillow under your thighs and/or forehead.
  • Extend your arms and breath deeply, hold this position for 3 to 5 minutes as per your convenience.
  • Even when you are doing some other stretches, you can perform this one to relax your body.

Low lunge

In addition to back and shoulders, this stretch is helpful for your hips, thighs, and growin. It is advisable not to push yourself too hard while performing this stretch.

  • Keep your right foot below your right knee and take the low lunge position.
  • Your left leg should extend backward and your knee should be on the floor.
  • Keep your hands on the floor and breathe deeply.
  • Focus on your breath, opening your chest, and lengthening your spine.
  • Repeat both sides and hold this position for 5 breaths.

Seated forward bend

From lower back to spine and shoulders to hamstrings, this particular stretch is great for various parts of your body.

  • Simply, sit on the floor and let your legs stretch in front of you.
  • Extend your arms and try reaching your feet, keep your head relaxed and let your chin tucked into your chest.
  • Hold this pose for 3 to 5 minutes.

Reclining bound angle

If you are among those who spend most of the day sitting, doing the desk job, this one can prove to be highly beneficial for you. This stretch can help you find relief from muscle tension.

  • You need to keep the sole of your feet together while sitting on the floor.
  • As you lean back, your back, hand, and neck should be on the floor.
  • Let your arms rest in a comfortable position.
  • Breath deeply and ensure that your hips and thighs are in a relaxing position.
